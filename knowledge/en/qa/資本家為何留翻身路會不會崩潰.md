> **In 30 seconds**: Capitalism is a **system designed by capitalists to sustain themselves** — but it's **fair and open, and can't fully seal off workers' class mobility** — so a path to wealth exists; it's just **"not advertised"**: schools don't teach it, only wealthy families, the awakened, and CLEC tell you. James's metaphor: **the system is a maze; capitalists have a navigation system, ordinary people don't — the "navigation" ordinary people get points them toward being workers. What CLEC gives you is that navigation.** Will it collapse because "everyone uses it"? **No** — try introducing this method to 100 people and see how many believe you: most think you're either a **scammer** or **money-crazed**. The vast majority **can't be woken**, so the system doesn't collapse.[^1]

## Why there IS a path: fair and open, but unadvertised

James compares capitalism to **"The Truman Show"** — from childhood you're penned into a worker's environment, raised into a worker without realizing it:[^1]

- The system **was created for capitalists**; "of course capitalists educate everyone to be workers — that's what's most advantageous to them."
- **But it can't fully seal off class mobility** — capitalism is **fair, just, and open** (the same point as [[勞工還是資本家|"wealth is a birthright"]]), it's simply **not advertised**. "The information handed to the marginal worker is, of all things, 'work hard as a worker.'"
- So the path to wealth has always been there; what's missing isn't the path but the **"telling"**: schools don't teach it, only **wealthy families, the awakened, and CLEC** do (echoing [[勞工還是資本家|"a capitalist doesn't need to be trained, only told"]]).

## The maze and the navigation: tell the "poison of exploitation" from the "elixir of wealth"

- **The system is a maze**: capitalists hold a navigation system; ordinary people don't — and the "navigation" they're handed **steers them toward being workers**.
- Inside the maze there's **both a system of exploitation and a road to wealth** — the question is whether you can tell **which is the "poison of exploitation" and which is the "elixir of wealth."**
- **What CLEC gives you is that navigation** — not a guarantee, but the ability to see, inside the maze, which road leads to being a capitalist and which locks you in the rat race ([[勞工還是資本家]]).

## Why it won't collapse: the vast majority can't be woken

"If everyone knew this path and stopped getting lost, wouldn't capitalism collapse?" — James says **no**:[^1]

- **Introduce this method to 100 people and see how many believe you**: most think you're either **scamming** them or **money-crazed**.
- "You couldn't wake them if you tried" — most are **deep in the maze, taking its hallucinogen**; that's human nature, not a shortage of information.
- This is the same truth as [[家人朋友賣掉了該怎麼辦|"even daily viewers can't do it; the truly wealthy are always a minority"]]: however open the method, **those willing to believe and follow through are always a tiny few** — so the system stays rock-solid and won't collapse because of CLEC.
