> **In 30 seconds**: This is CLEC's new-year "**first investing class**" and the channel's 101. The 2024 edition is titled *The Hundred-Million-Dollar Investment-Life Lecture* (video 00451); the 2026 edition expands into *The Ten-Billion-Dollar Investment Lecture*. Its core line: **"Wealth is a birthright; happiness is a human right."** It teaches you to leave the "worker's rat cage" and become a capitalist by **holding US index funds for the long term** (buy when you have money, never sell — just as a landlord never sells his land). Newcomers are often told "watch 00451 first" — this page is its overview and entry point.[^1]

> 📌 **This is an entry page, not a restatement**: each chapter's full content lives on its own concept page (links below); this page keeps only the lecture's **skeleton and order** and routes you onward. For numbers, the newer 2026 "ten-billion" edition governs on those concept pages.

## The lecture's skeleton (in deck order)

1. **A life worth living** — "wealth is a birthright, happiness is a human right." Wealth is a right you already have; you just need to be told. Ultimately you must feel happy. First ask: what is your most important life goal? And with a hundred million, what would it become?[^1] → [[快樂是人權]], [[別為錢賣命]]
2. **Capitalist vs. worker** — work is service, not for money; **only when you earn without labor and without spending time are you truly rich.** The capitalist gains freedom and fulfills a life mission, with money as a mere tool; the worker grinds 9-to-5, pays endless taxes and loans, trapped in the rat cage.[^1] → [[勞工還是資本家]]
3. **The worker's rat cage** — earning for the boss, trading life for money, paying taxes, begging for raises, earning to pay the bank's loans, buying a big house and car, material consumption, wasting resources, unable to escape the loop.[^1] → [[勞工還是資本家]], [[別為錢賣命]]
4. **The key to wealth: the conversion channel of money-energy** — the path beyond the rat cage is a chain: **learning/investing → living simply → saving hard → meeting basic material needs → savings → the market earns for me → giving back to society, lifting the collective's spirit-energy.** Earning by working is only the start; ultimately you let "the market (money) work for you automatically."[^1]
5. **The capitalist key to wealth** — **long-term investing in US index funds: buy when you have money, invest for the long term, never sell** (the same logic as a landlord never selling his land).[^1] → [[為什麼是納斯達克100]], [[從擇時操作到打死不賣]]
6. **Who must NOT invest** — **those who can't withstand market drops, and money needed short-term, should not enter.** A bubble can bring a 70% drop; workers should first keep about six months of emergency cash; anyone who'd worry themselves sleepless should keep money in fixed deposits — what you must avoid is bankruptcy, not the pursuit of wealth.[^1] → [[現金是空氣]], [[崩盤是朋友]]
7. **Instrument and discipline** — the core is simply the **Nasdaq-100 (QQQ) plus each country's corresponding local ETF**; don't accept any product an advisor pitches — **not even the ETFs/products a Charles Schwab financial consultant recommends after opening your account.**[^1] → [[全球納斯達克100指數基金對照]], [[值得讀的投資書籍]]

## Hundred-million → ten-billion: the same class, evolved

The 2024 edition is *The Hundred-**Million**-Dollar Investment-Life Lecture*; the 2026 edition expands into *The Ten-**Billion**-Dollar Investment Lecture* — the skeleton is unchanged (wealth is a birthright, leave the rat cage, hold the Nasdaq long-term), but it details the asset-allocation, pledging, and retirement-withdrawal chapters further. The channel treats it as the year's first class, and it's clec-md's flagship. For chapter detail see [[現金是空氣]], [[為什麼是納斯達克100]], [[勞工還是資本家]].[^1]
