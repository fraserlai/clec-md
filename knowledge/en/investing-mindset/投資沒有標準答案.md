> **In 30 seconds**: CLEC's "buy when you have money, never sell" is a **solid, stable cornerstone** — but it's the **starting point, not the endpoint** of investing. Once your skill is unshakeable, dare to **"forget the manual"** — because every school has strengths and weaknesses, **there is no most-perfect asset allocation; the most perfect one is always "customized,"** built from understanding your own personality, risk tolerance, and investing — uniquely yours. **No single answer fits everyone; no one can hand you a standard answer — only your own realization is "the Way."** And the opening move of critical thinking is to first tell yourself **"I might be wrong."**[^1]

## Reform: the cornerstone is a start, not an end

James calls this **"reform" (變革)** — after running CLEC for nearly eight years and investing for over 25, the key lesson at a certain stage is:[^1]

- When "buy when you have money, never sell" has become **unshakeable** for you, you've reached a high [[投資的七個層級|level]]. At this point, **stop clinging to the manual and grinding away** — once your skill has arrived, you must be able to forget it.
- The high-level person "**can accept the ocean and the void**" — you can see each school's strengths and its weaknesses, and you won't, like a half-full bucket, split hairs insisting "only I'm right and everyone else is wrong."
- **There is no most-perfect investment or asset allocation in the world.** The most perfect one is **customized**: an allocation built after understanding your investing, your personality, and your risk tolerance is the one that best suits you — uniquely yours.
- So "have the courage to embrace reform for life" — use this cornerstone to **build a tower and see a farther view**, rather than mistaking the cornerstone for the whole. (This is the same spirit as [[從擇時操作到打死不賣|from timing to never selling]]: the philosophy evolves.)

> 🧭 This is also why, in episode 00563, James walked everyone "once through cycles and rebalancing": not so you'd time cycles, but like a parent who'd rather the child **fall in front of them** than off a cliff — once you've felt the difficulty of rebalancing firsthand, you'll **all the more firmly confirm that "buy when you have money, never sell" is the best strategy.**

## Investing has no standard answer: only your own "realization" is "the Way"

- **Don't take every word James says as the only correct answer.** "Clinging to every word as literal truth makes you a pedant — it means you don't understand."
- Those who spread CLEC are "**evangelists**" — what they spread is **an idea, a philosophy**, not precise standard answers. Your investing life has **no one who can give you a standard answer.**
- **"Your food may be another's poison."** No single answer, and no single asset allocation, fits everyone. The truly correct answer is found **only by your own "realization"** — and the Way you realize won't be someone else's Way.
- It's clearest in the numbers: will you estimate QQQ's future return at 15%, 12%, 10%, or 8%? QQQI's dividend yield at 12%, 10%, 8%, or 6%? "**Only you have the answer; if you ask me, I don't have one.**" (For the conservative-estimation practice, see [[QQQI]].)

## Critical thinking: first say "I might be wrong"

James gives critical thinking a very concrete opening move:[^1]

- Before judging others or forming your own view, **first tell yourself "I might be wrong," "I'm wrong," "the other person is right"** — then think it through again. *That* is critical thinking.
- And if, after thinking, you find your way suits you better — **then keep it to yourself; you don't need to tell others.** "Telling others is useless, because it's what *you* like."

## Don't challenge others, don't argue

- **Challenging others reveals your own ignorance.** "If you truly understood, you wouldn't say that line — because you'd understand what they're saying, and see clearly where they're right and wrong, so you'd shut up. The ones who love to lecture others are usually half-full buckets."
- **Don't go comment-troll on others' channels**, and don't challenge the evangelists spreading CLEC; encourage and protect them, and respect others' speech.
- "**When you argue, no one can tell who the pig is**" / "don't wrestle with a pig" — arguing only drags your own level down. When someone who doesn't understand comes to troll under CLEC's channel, James simply blocks them and doesn't reply: no understanding, no need for dialogue.
- This connects to [[快樂是人權|"nothing exists, so why stir up dust"]] — save your energy for your own life, rather than burning it on arguing and comparison.
