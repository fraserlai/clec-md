Banks say their wealthy clients are borrowing more than ever before, often using loans backed by their portfolios of stocks and bonds.

For borrowers, the calculation is clear: If an asset appreciates faster than the appreciates faster than the interest rate on the loan, they come out ahead. And under current law, investors and their heirs don’t pay income taxes unless their shares are sold. The assets may be subject to estate taxes, but heirs pay capital gains taxes only when they sell and only on gains since the prior owner's death. The more they can borrow, the longer they can hold appreci- ating assets. And the longer they hold, the bigger the tax savings.

對於借款人來說，計算很清楚：如果資產的升值速度快於貸款利率的升值速度，他們就會領先。 根據現行法律，投資者及其繼承人無需繳納所得稅，除非他們的股票被出售。資產可能需要繳納遺產稅，但繼承人僅在出售時繳納資本利得稅，並且僅對前任所有者去世後的收益繳納。 他們借貸的越多，他們持有升值資產的時間就越長。 他們持有的時間越長，節省的稅收就越大。

Borrowers of securities- based loans face less red tape than someone looking for a mortgage or an auto loan. Paperwork is light and the debt often doesn’t show up on credit reports.
